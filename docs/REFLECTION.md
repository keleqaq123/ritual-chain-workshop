# Reflection

Bounty metadata, deadlines, reward amount, commitment hashes, and the final payout should be public because participants need to verify that the rules and settlement are fair. The answer itself should remain hidden during the submission phase, and in a stronger privacy design it should remain encrypted until the batch judging step is complete. Salts, decryption keys, private storage credentials, and any participant data not needed for verification should never be public on-chain. AI should compare all eligible answers against a predefined rubric, produce scores and reasons, and recommend a ranking in one batch. A human should retain the final decision when the rubric is subjective, when the AI output is malformed, or when policy concerns need contextual judgment. The contract should enforce objective rules such as timing, commitment validity, eligibility, and one-time payout. This division makes the system auditable without giving an LLM unchecked authority over funds.
